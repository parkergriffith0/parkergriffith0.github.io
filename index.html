About Me

Parker Griffith is a dedicated policy analyst focused on enhancing the welfare and safety of Detroit residents. As a Policy Analyst for Detroit Councilwoman Latisha Johnson, Parker conducts in-depth research and crafts policies that address the district's most pressing issues. His work involves preparing Councilwoman Johnson with detailed analyses and recommendations for key legislative decisions.

Parker has gained valuable experience through roles with the Economic Justice Alliance of Michigan and Local Initiatives Support Corporation (LISC) Detroit, where he contributed to strategic planning and economic development initiatives. He graduated summa cum laude from Wayne State University with a BA in Political Science and has participated in prestigious fellowships at the University of Michigan and Stanford University.

Beyond his professional accomplishments, Parker has shown leadership through his role as a Senior Resident Advisor at Wayne State University and his involvement in community projects as an Eagle Scout. Passionate about public service, Parker aspires to further his career in government oversight and accountability, with a long-term goal of making a lasting impact on his community.
